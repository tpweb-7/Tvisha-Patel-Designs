I design spaces that hold people — gently, boldly, and with intent.

I'm an interior architect working at the meeting point of biophilic principle and experimental form. My practice moves across hospitality, residential, and furniture design, with a recurring pull toward work that carries social weight — spaces for children leaving care, housing for migrant communities, schools that teach more than curriculum.

What stays constant across registers — a serene penthouse, a spice-warm Soho restaurant, a sculptural chair — is a confident point of view and the adaptability to meet each brief on its own terms. I believe great design is, first, an act of empathy.
